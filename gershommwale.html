Gershom Mwale

Lecturer, Researcher & Academic Mentor

Lusaka, Zambia
gershom@gershommwale.com
Department of Economics & Development Studies

Dr. Gershom Mwale is a dedicated academic, researcher, and mentor with over eight years of experience in higher education. His work bridges economic theory, development policy, and educational reform, focusing on the Zambian and broader Southern African context. He holds advanced degrees in Economics and Education Policy, and has published extensively in peer-reviewed journals on topics ranging from fiscal policy to curriculum innovation.

Beyond scholarship, Gershom is deeply invested in building a culture of intellectual curiosity among his students, mentoring emerging researchers, and contributing to evidence-based policy dialogue that shapes the future of education and economic development in Africa.

Focus Areas

Research Interests

Education Policy & Finance

Public investment in education, policy reform, curriculum design, and institutional effectiveness in Sub-Saharan Africa.

Development Economics

Human capital formation, poverty reduction, and inclusive economic growth through education and skills development.

Fiscal Policy & Governance

Public expenditure management, budget allocation for social sectors, and government accountability mechanisms.

Labour Markets & Employment

Skills mismatch, graduate employability, informal economy dynamics, and workforce transition in African economies.

Higher Education Systems

University governance, academic quality assurance, student access and equity, and internationalisation.

Quantitative Research Methods

Econometric modelling, panel data analysis, impact evaluation, and mixed-methods research design.
